Now, we will describe all the Marvel movies mentioned in the above list with a brief description of each of them.

1. Iron Man (2008)

Iron Man was the first release of Marvel Studio's movies, released in the year 2008. Although it was not as successful as the prior franchise of Spider-Man, however, it was still capable of putting an impression. With a revenue of $585.2 million, it became the eighth-highest-grossing film of the year.

2. The Incredible Hulk (2008)

The Incredible Hulk casting Edward Norton was the next release of the Marvel series after Iron Man. It was improved as compared to its precursors and earned $263.4 million.

The achievements of Iron Man had an impact on the popularity of the movie. However, it was understandable who would be Marvel's flagship character among these two. However, the character gained enough achievements and fruition by doing subsidiary roles in various movies.

3. Iron Man 2 (2010)

Marvel Studios brought a movie in 2010, Iron Man 2. The direction was done by Jon Favreau, and the script was prepared by Justin Theroux. The movie earned $623.9 million at the box office, becoming the seventh highest-grossing film of the year. But it was the ultimate approval of the superhero in 2010. Unluckily, the common accord of Iron Man 2 was that it could not match the impact of the first film's popularity.

4. Thor (2011)

Dissimilar to the Iron Man movies, Thor was not the best-grossing released film of 2011. The film made a revenue of $449.3 million and proceeded ahead of the Hulk and Captain America. Critics applauded Chris Hemsworth's work in the film, and some were still questioning the capability of Marvel's franchise. They marked the shortcomings in Thor, which made Iron Man more splendid.

5. Captain America- The First Avenger (2011)

Marvel Studio released its fifth movie also in 2011, i.e., 'Captain America- The First Avenger.' Chris Evans played Captain America as the lead role in the movie, and this movie marked the entrance of Captain America into the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). So, it may surprise many new Marvel fans, but yes, Thor, Hulk, and Iron Man all have their movies before Captain America.

6. The Avengers (2012)

Finally, Marvel Studios came up with "The Avengers" in 2012. The movie reached the peak of success at the box office and left behind many of its tough competitors, including "The Dark Knight Rises," which was a sequel to Iron Man. The movie became the first Marvel movie that earned the proofing billion dollars, i.e., 1.5 billion dollars. The profit and success earned by the film are still unmatched until the release of "Avenger- Infinity War" after six years.

7. Iron Man 3 (2013)

The movie made a revenue of 1.2 billion dollars and became the second movie to cross the billion dollars earning. Shane Black made a debut as a director that brought a big evolution of Tony Stark.

Finally, the critics were satisfied with the storyline and applauded the movie as betterment of Iron Man 2, which was much better in the action-comedy genre. The movie was the biggest blockbuster of the year.

8. Thor: The Dark World (2013)

The movie made a revenue of $644.6 million at the box office and became the tenth highest-grossing year of the year and was far better than the original one leaving behind Iron Man 2. Even after the ill-famed reputation of the film, it was not possible for the viewers to go and watch the movie.

9. Captain America: The Winter Soldier

Captain America received mixed interest and positive feedback. The movie established itself as the powerhouse of the Marvel series with a box office gross of $714.3 million. Critics applauded the film and its political subtext, which is only attempted by very few superhero movies.

The direction of the Russo Brothers and the script written by Christopher Markus and Stephen McFeely did a phenomenal job, and the results were proof of that.

10. Guardians of the Galaxy (2014)

In 2014, Guardians of the Galaxy was a movie that was highly successful in pushing the boundaries of viewers' interest. The film gathered $773.3 million, which was way too higher than any Thor or Captain America.

Transformers: Age of Extinction and 'The Hobbit and The Battle of the five Armies' were only the two stiff competitors. The critics felt a connection with the film's irrelevant humour and elegance and also with the sentiments possessed by its unusual superheroes.

11. Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015)

The Age of Ultron was the movie that made Marvel Studios listed in the billion-dollar empire once again. The film accumulated $1.4 billion, which was lesser than the first Avengers but still exists in the territory of cinematic kingship.

Critics were not connected enough with the movie than the first movie. However, they adored the character interaction and the heroic moments in the movie.

12. Ant-Man (2015)

The Ant-Man became the next big release of the Marvel studios in order. The studio had high expectations from the movie that it would springboard the office of Ultron's profit that was fulfilled by the movie. It made revenue of $519.3 million which was not enough earnings for the second phase. Either it was fatigue, or the marketing was unable to sell out the concepts as well as it did for the Guardians of the Galaxy.

Though, critics were fond of the Ant-Man and marked that the reduced scale of the movie performed in its goodwill and revived behind Paul Rudd's like potential as a well-intended but fighting father.

13. Captain America: Civil War (2016)

Captain America: Civil War boosted the star-studded potential of an Avenger movie. The movie has beaten both Batman vs Superman: Dawn of Justice and Rogue One: A Star Wars Story and was declared the number-one movie of the year at the box office.

With earnings of $1.2 billion dollars, Captain America: Civil War became the fourth movie to accumulate a billion dollars in earnings.

14. Doctor Strange (2016)

The movie was the second of the Third phase of the Marvel series. This was the longest phase that was fully boasted by various latest characters. Doctor Strange gathered $677.7 million at the box office. Marvel Cinematic Universe was pushed into a new direction with its Doctor Strange, which showed some very new plots and concepts. The story of the movie was mainly based on a doctor named Stephen Strange who had a car accident and how their life changed from thereon.

15. Guardians of the Galaxy Volume 2

The Marvel movies gained achievements with two films per year, but in 2017 they added one more achievement to their schedule by coming up with three movies per year.

The second volume marginally better gathered a profit than the first volume, i.e., collection of $863.6 million. These were not the Civil War numbers, but they carried on with being the achievement of the ragtag privilege. The Guardians showed the viewers their reliability even more than Thor. 16. Spider-Man Homecoming (2017)

Spider-Man Homecoming is an amazing movie. The earliest Spider-Man movie, released in 2002, made a revenue of $821.1 million which was boosted up to $890.9 million with the launch of Spider-Man 3.

Later, the profit was brought down to $700 million by "The Amazing Spider-Man"; it wasn't that bad but a matter of concern for Sony.

By good fortune, Spider-Man Homecoming, the loss was healed with a profit of $880.2 million. Though the earning was not more than Spider-Man 3, it still gave good feedback than "The Amazing Spider-Man" and became the top-grossing superhero movie in 2017

17. Thor: Ragnarök (2017)

Thor: Ragnarök stood at the third position in the success order of the three Marvel movies that were released in 2017. But the profit it made was $854 million and is no less than a prize for Marvel Studios.

Taika Waititi, the director of the film, was successful in making the film stand apart.

Critics totally enjoyed the comic direction of the film. The movie gained much success, and not only this, it encouraged Chris Hemsworth to do the next project of Waititi, "Thor: Love and Thunder".

18. Black Panther (2018)

Black Panther was adored by the Critics. The critics admired it for utilizing the superhero figure to spread a political message that reverberated with the present-time viewers. The movie was impactful and had certain significance to African and African - American viewers who viewed their honest reflections of themselves in the mythical stories of superheroes.

The popularity was clearly visible at the box office, with a revenue of $1.35 billion, setting it in the billion dollars zone.

At the end of 2018, the movie established itself as the second top-grossing movie of the year and got nominated for the BEST PICTURE award, which solidified its significance to the audience and the company.

The movie shattered the standard gross of all the movies launched by marvel studios by generating a revenue of $ 2 billion at the box office. The movie abolished past "The Avengers" to become the fourth top-grossing movie of all time.

Ant-Man and the Wasp gathered $ 622 million across the world, which was much more than the original Ant-Man but a marked fall after the great success of Infinity War and Black Panther. Even the film was unable to match the level of Thor: The Dark World's grossing at the box office.

2019 was the seller year for Disney, and Captain Marvel was the ace player in that. Because of the pre-sale numbers, it was behind Black Panther and Infinity War; otherwise, it is the top-listed movie of the Marvel Cinematic Universe movies. The movie accumulated $1.128 billion at the box office.

This was the first time when all three movies of the Marvel studios in order smashed billion of dollars in earnings in a year. The Spider-Man: Far from Homemade, a profit of $1.1 billion. Sony was credited for the success of the movie but pulled from Disney's Endgame was a motivation.

WandaVision was the first television series of Marvel Studios streamed originally on the Disney+ Hotstar platform, and it was the 24th instalment of MCU that premiered its first episode in 2021. 27. Black Widow (2021)

Based on the same-named heroine from Marvel Comics, Black Widow is a 2021 superhero movie. Both a prequel and a follow-up to Captain America: Civil War is provided by this film. It is both the fourth Phase Four film and the twenty-fourth movie in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. The film premiered in theatres and on Disney+ with Premier Access on 9 July 2021.

Natasha Romanoff, nicknamed Black Widow, confronts the darker sides of her background when a perilous plot with links to her past emerges in Marvel Studios' action-packed spy thriller Black Widow. Since a force is after her and will stop at nothing to bring her down, Natasha must deal with her history as a spy and the shattered relationships she left in her wake before she joined the Avengers.

28. What If…? (2021)

What If...? turns the MCU on its head by reinterpreting well-known scenes from the movies in novel ways. Marvel Studios' first animated series focuses on numerous characters from the MCU and features a voice cast that features a plethora of performers that reprise previous roles. 29. Eternals (2021)

The 2021 superhero film Eternals is based on the Marvel Comics race of the same name. The Marvel Cinematic Universe, which was created by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures, now has 26 films (MCU).

Ten very powerful Eternals, Ajak, Sersi, Ikaris, Kingo, Sprite, Phastos, Makkari, Druig, Gilgamesh, and Thena, are sent to Earth in 5000 BC by the Celestial Arishem on their spaceship, the Domo, to exterminate the invading Deviants. The last Deviants were purportedly killed in 1521 due to internal conflict about how they should continue to interact with humans. During the following 500 years, they primarily lived apart while they waited for orders from Arishem.

Now, Sersi and Sprite live together in London. After her old partner, Ikaris abandoned her decades ago, Sersi has been dating human Dane Whitman, who works at the Natural History Museum. The Deviant Kro assaults the three, but Ikaros arrives and chases it away. As the three Eternals arrive in South Dakota to meet up with their commander, Ajak, they discover that she has passed away. Ajak designates Sersi as her heir after her passing, giving her access to Arishem.

30. Hawkeye (2021)

Based on the Marvel Comics superhero Hawkeye, Jonathan Igla (the head writer) and Rhys Thomas, who is leading the directorial crew, have produced the action-adventure TV miniseries Hawkeye, which is currently available to stream on Disney+.

The Marvel Cinematic Universe's sixth TV series was created by Marvel Studios. It shows what happens after Endgame, with characters like Clint Barton and Kate Bishop at the centre. It's like giving Marvel's (Avengers) fans a holiday present, especially for fans of Hawkeye. This series differs significantly from the other MCU movies and shows.

The first episode opens with the alien invasion of New York in 2012. Young Kate was saved by Clint at this time, and she also saw Clint (Hawkeye) defeat the Chitauri with just a bow and arrow. She was emotionally moved and impressed by his deeds, designating him as her favourite superhero. She then made the decision to learn archery. When attending a charity event, time jumps to the present (2024, according to the plot).

Conversely, Clint Barton (Hawkeye) is planning and making every effort to spend quality time with his family during the Christmas season, but things don't turn out as he had hoped. He has to deal with and complete what he started, the enemies he made, and the repercussions of his previous life as a Ronin. Clint Barton finds Kate Bishop, the recipient of the Ronin's suit, and later teams up with her to deal with a number of issues, including the Tracksuit Mafia Gang, the murder of Armand Duquesne III, and Yelena Belova, the Black Widow, whose goal is to kill Clint, while also enjoying a joyous holiday celebration. Clint does his best to deal with all of the issues and return to his family in time for Christmas.

31. Spider-Man: No Way Home

Spider-Man: No Way Home is a superhero movie from 2021 that is based on the Marvel Comics superhero with the same title. The movie is a crossover/sequel to the Spider-Man trilogy and The Amazing Spider-Man duology, as well as a sequel to Spider-Man: Homecoming and Spider-Man: Far From Home. It is the twenty-seventh film overall and the ninth film in phase Four in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. On 17 December 2021, the movie was shown in the US. On 2 September 2022, a longer version of the movie with more footage, dubbed Spider-Man: No Way Home - The More Fun Things Version, was shown in theatres for one week.

For the first time in Spider-Man movie history, our amiable neighbourhood hero's identity is revealed, interfering with his daily tasks and endangering those closest to him. As he begs Doctor Strange to assist him in recovering his secret, the spell rips a hole in their world and releases the most powerful foes to oppose a Spider-Man in any universe. Peter must now overcome his hardest challenge so far, which will forever alter not just the fate of the Multiverse but also his own future.

32. Moon Knight (2022)

Based on the Marvel Comics superhero of the same name, Moon Knight is an American web television series. It is the eighteenth television series in the Marvel Cinematic Universe and the tenth movie in Phase Four. The programme was made by Marvel Studios, and it launched on Disney+.

The first and only season lasted from 30 March 2022 until 4 May 2022.

The main character of the programme is Steven Grant, a pleasant gift store employee who begins experiencing blackouts and flashbacks to a past life. Steven discovers that Marc Spector, an assassin, shares his body and that he suffers from a dissociative identity disorder. When Steven/foes Marc move in on them, they are thrown into a dangerous mystery among the powerful gods of Egypt and must navigate their murky identities.

33. Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022)

A superhero movie from 2022 called Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness is based on the same-named character from Marvel Comics. The movie is a follow-up to WandaVision, Doctor Strange, and Avengers: Endgame. It is the eleventh movie in phase Four and the twenty-eighth movie overall in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. The release day for the movie was 6 May 2022.

Strange enlists the assistance of his friend Wong, the Sorcerer Supreme, and Wanda, the most potent Scarlet Witch in the Avengers, to restore a world where everything is changing. Nevertheless, a dreadful peril that can no longer be dealt with by their might alone hangs over mankind and the whole cosmos. What's more shocking is that Dr Strange resembles the worst menace in the cosmos.

34. Thor: Love and Thunder (2022)

Thor: Love and Thunder is a superhero film from 2022 that is based on the same-named character from Marvel Comics. The film serves as a prequel to Thor, Thor: The Dark World, Thor: Ragnarok, and Avengers: Endgame. It is the twenty-ninth film overall and the thirteenth film in Phase Four of the Marvel Cinematic Universe. The release day for the movie was 8 July 2022.

In the film, Thor sets out on an inner peace quest, unlike anything he has ever done. But his retirement is disturbed by Gorr, the God Butcher, an assassin from another galaxy who intends to exterminate all gods. To fight the threat, Thor enlists the help of King Valkyrie, Korg, and his ex-girlfriend Jane Foster. Jane Foster, the Mighty Thor, magically holds his magical hammer, Mjolnir, to Thor's surprise. Together, they go on a risky cosmic adventure to find out what caused the God Butcher's retribution and kill him before it's too late.

35. I Am Groot (2022)

36. She-Hulk: Attorney at Law
